1. Scope

This Privacy Policy describes how ALAS Browser ("ALAS Browser", "we", "our", or "us") collects, uses, discloses, and safeguards information when you use the ALAS Browser mobile application and related services (collectively, the "Service").

2. Data We Collect

2.1 Account and Sign-In Data

If you create an in-app account profile, the app stores your email address and display name locally on your device. If you choose Google sign-in, Google Credential Manager and related Google identity services may provide your email address, display name, profile image, and authentication tokens. Depending on the configured build, Google or Firebase identity services may also be used to complete sign-in and maintain your account session.

2.2 Locally Stored Browser Data

The following information may be stored locally on your device depending on your settings and usage:

  • Browsing history, open tabs, bookmarks, and quick links
  • Website cookies, cache, and local website storage
  • Saved passwords and autofill data if you enable those features
  • Downloaded files, download history, and app preferences

2.3 Local Storage and Future Sync

Current builds keep bookmarks, tabs, browsing history, downloads, and selected settings on your device. Sync is planned for a future update and is not an active user feature right now. If optional encrypted sync or backup is added later, this page will be updated to describe how it works before or when that feature becomes available.

2.4 Technical, Analytics, and Service Data

We may process limited technical data required to operate the Service, such as app version, device type, operating system version, network status, error diagnostics, and feature configuration. Some builds may also use Google or Firebase services for sign-in, analytics, diagnostics, or related operations. The app may fetch remote filter lists or site-compatibility rules and cache them locally to improve browsing behavior.

3. How We Use Data

We process data to:

  • Provide browsing, account sign-in, password/autofill, and download features
  • Store, restore, or delete local browser data and account profile information
  • Maintain app stability, performance, security, and site compatibility
  • Diagnose problems and improve service reliability
  • Detect abuse, fraud, and unauthorized activity
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce terms

4. Legal Bases for Processing

Where applicable under data protection laws, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases: performance of a contract, legitimate interests, legal obligations, and your consent (where required).

5. Data Sharing and Processors

We do not sell personal data. We may share data with service providers acting on our behalf, including Google or Firebase services used for sign-in, analytics, diagnostics, or other configured service functionality. Third-party websites, search engines, media providers, and download sources also receive the network requests needed to serve the content you choose to access.

6. International Transfers

Your data may be processed in countries other than your own, including where our service providers operate. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards for cross-border data transfers.

7. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to provide the Service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Local account and browser data remains on your device until you delete it, sign out, delete the account, or uninstall the app, subject to Android backup and restore behavior. Files you downloaded to shared storage remain until you remove them manually.

8. Security Measures

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures can include platform-provided credential protection and local storage safeguards where those features are used. No security method is completely infallible.
